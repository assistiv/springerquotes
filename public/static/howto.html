How does this work?

This application allows you to directly link to a paragraph of a Springer article or to highlight individual sentences. This makes it more convenient to share information with fellow researchers and to point them directly to specific portions of an article. This could as well be used to directly link to when you are citing from a scientific paper.

How to Link to a Paragraph?

  • Double-tap the Shift key.
    Paragraph symbols ¶ will appear next to each paragraph. Click any paragraph symbol to generate a link to that paragraph. The link will appear in your browser’s address bar.
  • Click on the 'Share Quotes' button at the top of the screen to share are direct link to this paragraph.
    The URL will automatically be updated as you click around, so it always reflects the current appearance of the page.

How to Highlight Sentences?

  • Double-tap the Shift key.
    Paragraph symbols ¶ will appear next to each paragraph.
  • Click a paragraph.
    A light gray shading will be applied to the text of the paragraph, indicating that it’s the currently selected paragraph.
  • Click a sentence within the currently selected paragraph.
    This toggles an orange highlighting on the sentence (click a few different sentences to see how it works).
  • Click a different paragraph.
    You’ll see the gray shading again, confirming that the new paragraph is the active paragraph.
  • Click on the 'Share Quotes' button at the top of the screen to share are direct link to this paragraph.
    The URL will automatically be updated as you click around, so it always reflects the current appearance of the page.
